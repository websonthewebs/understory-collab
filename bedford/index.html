“The best burger isn’t the most famous one. It’s the one where someone cared enough to get every detail right — and you could taste the difference.”

Bedford feels like that kind of project.

People who can taste the difference between good and great are exactly the people who get frustrated building programs. The gap between a learner experience that feels right and one that actually holds people is almost invisible from the outside, and it becomes brutally obvious once you’re inside it.

Onboarding is where most programs lose people. The content is usually fine. The experience around it was treated as an afterthought rather than designed with care.
